FAQ

Your questions answered

I've put together a list of some questions you might have.

If you can't find the answer here, please don't hesitate to contact me.

General

How does it work?

Firstly,contact me via the contact page to begin the initial consultation phase. Tell me as much as you can about what you'd for your site, and I'll get back to you as soon as possible.

  • IMPORTANT: the more detail you can give me at this stage, the smoother the process will be. Changes later can cost extra.

When I have a clear understanding of your needs, I'll send you a contract to sign. Once that contract has been signed, I'll begin work on your website, and I'll keep you updated on the progress. This is when you'd send me any content you want on the website, such as images and text.

When the site has been built, I'll send you a link to take a look. At this stage, I'm happy to make non-structural changes, such as colours, effects, images, etc. However, if you'd like me to make any structural changes, such as new pages or changing the page layout, that will incur an additional fee. This is why it's so important to be as detailed as possible during the initial consultation phase, as anything agreed upon during that phase will be included in the ongoing monthly fee.

After any changes have been made, the site will go live soon thereafter. If you have a domain you'd like me to connect to the website, I can do that for you too.

And... that's it! You'll be able to make unlimited content changes (such as images or text) to the site going forward simply by emailing me. I'll make sure to monitor the site regularly, to ensure everything is running smoothly. If everything goes according to plan, you should never have to worry about your website ever again.

And how does the monthly plan work?

You pay a single monthly fee, and that's it! No hidden or extra costs. Unless you decide to add pages or change the structure of the website, the monthly fee is all you'll pay.

Is there really no upfront fee?

That's correct! With the exception of some optional add-ons, which are not included in the standard packages, there is no upfront fee. Once you sign the contract, the monthly fee is all you need to pay.

Do I have to sign a long-term contract?

No. All contracts are month-to-month. When you pay, you're paying for the following month, so if you decide to stop at any point, you can without any cancellation penalties. All I ask is that you give me thirty days notice before you intend to cancel.

Please note that if you cancel without giving notice and decide to re-activate your website again in the future, you will be required to pay for the months between your initial cancellation and your request for re-activation before the site will be re-activated.

What happens if I cancel?

The website will remain online until the end of your billing period, at which point it'll be taken offline.

Who actually owns the website?

Unless you decide to pay the Fixed Buyout Fee, which will be specified in your contract, I retain ownership of the site. However, the ownership of the domain name, as well as any images or content you provide to me will, of course, remain yours.

Can I buy the website outright later?

Absolutely! In your contract, there will be a Fixed Buyout Fee specified. If you decide to pay that fee at any time, you will become the sole owner of the entire website. If you decide to pay that fee and want me to continue to host and manage your site, I can do that too. But that would be up to you!

Pricing & Billing

When are payments taken each month?

Payments are taken each month on or around the date your first payment was made. So, if you signed up on 23 November, then all subsequent payments will be taken on or around the 23rd of each month going forward.

Do prices ever change?

With the exception of the annual price adjustment, which I explain in another question on this page, your monthly fee will not increase.

What's the “Initial Discount Period”?

This is a fixed period of time (usually twelve months) where your monthly fee is reduced. It's that simple! Once that period comes to an end, the price will increase to the standard monthly rate for the tier at that time.

How does the annual price adjustment work?

Each year in April, I may increase the monthly rate, either by £5, or the UK Consumer Price Index (CPI) plus 3%, whichever is greater. If the CPI method is used, I will use the figure from the preceding December.

What happens if my payment fails?

If a payment fails, the payment system will automatically attempt to make the payment three times: two, five, and seven days later. If the payment fails after the third attempt, the site will be taken offline and I will treat is as a cancellation without notice.

If you think you're not going to be able to pay, please let me know. The site will still be taken offline, but you won't need to make any extra payments once you're able to pay to have it back online, whereas you would if you didn't notify me.

What happens if I stop paying?

Your website will be taken offiline.

If you give me notice beforehand, then you won't be required to make any payments if you'd like to re-activate the site again in the future.

If you stop payment without notifying me and wish to re-activate the site again in the future, then you'll be required to pay for the months the site was offline before the site will be re-activated.

Is VAT included in the price?

At present, I'm not registered for VAT, so VAT is not charged on any of my prices. Should I become VAT-registered in the future, I'll inform all clients in advance, and any changes to pricing will be communicated clearly before taking effect.

What is your refund policy?

I provide ongoing website design, hosting, and maintenance services on a subscription basis. Because these services begin immediately and involve continuous time, hosting, and resources, all payments are non-refundable once collected. This also means that the statutory 14-day cooling-off period under the Consumer Contracts Regulations 2013 does not apply once the contract has been signed.

Of course, if there's ever an issue or you're unhappy with my work, please reach out. I'll always do my best to make it right.
